Quadrotor
December, 2013

Through a Mechanical Engineering seminar during my first semester at Cal, I got together with three classmates to build a quadrotor. I'm really excited about this project because I feel that these computer enabled aircrafts have huge potential to change the way we think of location - making it less and less relevent to our decision making as we move forward. The small, light-weight, and low-cost design of these rotorcrafts presents a host of opportunities for innovation.

We're building the thing using the BeagleBone as a microcontroller and a combination of 3-D printed and water jetted parts, as well as some parts from an old quadrotor.

We are still starting to get the project off the ground and are learning how to work with the Beaglebone as well as preparing parts.

*Image courtesy of The Stanford/Berkeley Testbed of Autonomous Rotorcraft for Multi-Agent Control (STARMAC).

Hando
November, 2013

At the Hackers@Berkeley Hackathan this Fall, a few of us got together and thought about what to build. This was the first hackathon any of us had attended. We couldn't agree or decide on much besides one thing; we all often lost track of where our keys were.

With that, we decided to make a web app that would allow us to open our doors remotely, from our phones, tablets or laptops. What resulted was Hando, an application that does exactly that - remotely unlocks a door.

We used a BeagleBone microcontroller to drive a motor that would attach onto a door on the inside and open it by reeling in a cable attached to the door knob, as well as some HTML/CSS and Bootstrap templates for the frontend, and Python on Flask for the backend.

Unfortunately, the whole set-up had to be taken down and it wasn't very pretty. We won “Best First Time Hack”.

Check out the demo and source code !

Scheme Interpreter
November, 2013

Interpreter for the Scheme programming language, built with Python. Check out the code!

If you'd like to try it out on your own machine, feel free to download. Then, cd into the appropriate directory in your terminal and run the file scheme.py with the command "python3 scheme.py" to interact with scheme command line.
